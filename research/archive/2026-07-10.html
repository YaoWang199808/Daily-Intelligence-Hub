Extreme reciprocal asymmetric elastic wave energy transport enabled by an ultra-thin, non-deflecting Willis structure

Authors: Chan Wook Park, Chan Wook Park, Gi Rim Nam, Yurim Lee, Jun Hyeong Park, Myung Hwan Bae, Choon-Su Park, Choon-Su Park, Hong Min Seung

Institution: Korea Research Institute of Standards and Science, Korea University of Science and Technology

Published: 2026-07-09

Journal: Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Signal rectification protects sensor systems that are vulnerable to external disturbances and enables diode-based signal control. Achieving such direction-dependent energy transport, however, typically requires breaking reciprocity through cumbersome devices, which limits practical feasibility. In addition, metamaterial- or diffraction-based approaches often rely on bulky structures or deflect the wave propagation direction, with wave deflection compromising signal fidelity. Therefore, realizing strong asymmetric energy transport in a compact, non-deflecting, and reciprocal device remains elusive. Here, we present a subwavelength mode-coupled Willis-type structure that enables highly asymmetric elastic wave transmission by fundamentally engineering the elastic constitutive relations. By identifying and enhancing the mode coupled Willis coefficients, which are otherwise weak, we establish a systematic route for controlling direction dependent intermodal energy exchange between longitudinal and shear wave modes. Leveraging this mechanism, we demonstrate unidirectional perfect transmission and reflection, in which complete mode converting transmission occurs for one sided incidence while total reflection arises for the opposite direction, all while preserving reciprocity. Ultrasonic experiments in a plate confirm unidirectional perfect mode-converting transmission and reflection for shear-horizontal wave incidence, in good agreement with numerical simulations. Unlike previous approaches, the proposed ultra-thin structure achieves strong asymmetric energy transport while preserving the signal direction and reciprocity, thereby enabling reliable signal communication. As potential applications, we further demonstrate elastic wave diodes and signal isolation, highlighting the practical utility of mode coupled Willis structures for directional wave control.

Towards a more realistic evaluation of machine learning models for bearing fault diagnosis

Authors: João Paulo Abreu Vieira, Victor Afonso Bauler, Rodrigo Kobashikawa Rosa, Danilo Silva

Institution: Universidade Federal de Santa Catarina

Published: 2026-07-08

Journal: Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 1

Abstract:

Reliable detection of bearing faults is essential for maintaining the safety and operational efficiency of rotating machinery. While recent advances in machine learning (ML), particularly deep learning, have shown strong performance in controlled settings, many studies fail to generalize to real-world applications due to methodological flaws, most notably data leakage. This paper investigates the issue of data leakage in vibration-based bearing fault diagnosis and its impact on model evaluation. We demonstrate that common dataset partitioning strategies, such as segment-wise and condition-wise splits, introduce spurious correlations that inflate performance metrics. To address this, we propose a rigorous, leakage-free evaluation methodology centered on bearing-wise data partitioning, ensuring no overlap between the physical components used for training and testing. Additionally, we reformulate the classification task as a multi-label problem, enabling the detection of co-occurring fault types and the use of prevalence-independent metrics based on the ROC curve. Beyond preventing leakage, we also examine the effect of dataset diversity on generalization, showing that the number of unique training bearings is a decisive factor for achieving robust performance. We evaluate our methodology on four widely adopted datasets: Case Western Reserve University (CWRU), Paderborn University (PU), University of Ottawa (UORED-VAFCLS) and Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST bearing). This study highlights the importance of leakage-aware evaluation protocols and provides practical guidelines for dataset partitioning, model selection, and validation, fostering the development of more trustworthy ML systems for industrial fault diagnosis applications.

Machine learning-based prediction and analysis of dimensional accuracy in fused deposition modelling of Nylon-Aramid fibre composite parts

Authors: Kallambadi Sadashivappa Prashanth, Nagendra Jayaram, Alok Kumar, Sugandha Saxena, Din Bandhu, H. V. BALACHANDRA ACHAR

Institution: Centre for Artificial Intelligence and Robotics, Galgotias University, Dr. Hari Singh Gour University, Manipal Academy of Higher Education

Published: 2026-07-08

Journal: Measurement

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Fused Deposition Modelling (FDM) of fibre‑reinforced composites offers enhanced mechanical properties but introduces thermal and rheological interactions that compromise dimensional accuracy. Nylon–Aramid short‑fibre composites are promising, yet their dimensional behavior under varying process parameters remains uncharacterized. Most machine learning (ML) studies on FDM accuracy rely on in‑sample metrics or k‑fold cross‑validation, leading to overoptimistic generalization estimates. A Taguchi L 27 array varied five FDM parameters (layer thickness 0.20–0.40 mm, print temperature 280–300 °C, raster angle 0–90°, infill pattern, infill density 70–90%) for a Nylon‑Aramid (98/2 wt%) composite. Dimensional deviations (length, width, thickness, diameter) were measured. Five ML models, Gaussian Process Regression (GPR), Support Vector Regression (SVR), Random Forest (RF), XGBoost, and Artificial Neural Network (ANN), were trained and evaluated under leave‑one‑out cross‑validation (LOO‑CV). XGBoost achieved the best generalization (mean RMSE = 0.02658, mean MAPE = 37.65%). Feature importance showed print temperature as dominant for length deviation, while infill density drove width, thickness, and diameter deviations, departing from Taguchi rankings. Parametric effect curves revealed a non‑monotonic temperature dependence (optimal near 285–292 °C) for length deviation. The optimal combined setting (0.20 mm, 290 °C, 45°, triangular infill, 70% density) was Experiment 4, which had the lowest combined dimensional accuracy index. This first ML‑based dimensional accuracy study for Nylon‑Aramid FDM composites shows that XGBoost with LOO‑CV provides robust generalization on small DOE datasets. The identified optima offer actionable guidance for process engineers.

Cold-formed shear walls with burring holes for the seismic strengthening of existing reinforced concrete buildings

Authors: Michelle Gualdi, Andrea Belleri, Simone Labò, Atsushi Sato

Institution: University of Bergamo, Nagoya Institute of Technology

Published: 2026-07-02

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Decarbonization is a key priority in the global effort to promote sustainable development and mitigate climate change. A significant contribution can be made by renovating the existing European building stock, which is largely characterized by low energy efficiency and inadequate structural performance. To address this challenge, integrated and sustainable renovation strategies based on Life Cycle Thinking principles have been proposed, aiming to enhance both structural safety and energy performance. In this paper, cold-formed steel panels with burring holes are proposed as earthquake-resistant elements for the integrated renovation of existing reinforced concrete (RC) buildings. These shear walls are used in Japan as lateral force-resistant systems for new lightweight buildings. The proposed retrofit solution offers key advantages, including lightness, ease of installation, and the possibility of disassembly and component reuse, thereby enhancing overall sustainability. Three finite element modelling strategies with varying levels of complexity are presented to capture the behaviour observed in previous experimental campaigns. A design methodology for a retrofit solution for RC buildings is developed, incorporating the stringer-panel model to derive forces in individual elements. Finally, both the modelling approach and design method are validated through application to a reference case study building representative of existing RC structures not designed according to earthquake-resistant provisions. Vulnerability analyses have been performed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the designed retrofit solution involving perforated metal panels.
