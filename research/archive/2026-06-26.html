Ecologically relevant classification of night-time lighting using an RGB aware, event-scale approach

Authors: Paweł Tysiąc, Michael D. Wood, Dan E-Nilsson, Artur Janowski, Łukasz Janowski, Alejandro Sánchez de Miguel, Camila Palhares Teixeira, Vinícius Donisete Lima Rodrigues Goulart, Alexandre Túlio Amaral Nascimento, Reinhard Klenke

Institution: Gdańsk University of Technology, University of Salford, Lund University, Gdynia Maritime University, Universidade Federal do Estado do Rio de Janeiro, Luther University, German Centre for Integrative Biodiversity Research, Martin Luther University Halle-Wittenberg

Published: 2026-06-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Review / Survey

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Artificial light at night (ALAN) alters nocturnal ecosystems through both intensity and spectrum, yet routine mapping of ecologically relevant light fields at scales appropriate for management decision making remains elusive. We present LumiMaSAM, a probabilistic indicator that translates aerial night-time Red, Green, and Blue (RGB) imagery and Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR) derived structure into decision-support maps of dominant spectral regimes with per-pixel uncertainty. By fusing physics guided similarities Mahalanobis Distance for intensity-sensitive reliability and Spectral Angle Mapper for brightness-invariant spectral shape-with a discriminative classifier the method delivers reliability-qualified proxies with per-pixel uncertainty. LiDAR-derived terrain layers supply structural priors that suppress implausible colour transitions at canopy edges and in self-shadow zones, where traditional nadir-based model fail. Applied to a pilot study during music festival with lighting features, LumiMaSAM produced maps within a high-confidence mask that isolate Blue/Red spill paths, under-canopy reflections, and Dark corridors at sub-meter resolution (25 cm), enabling luminaire-scale hypotheses (shielding, re-aiming, spectrum tuning, dimming) to be tested. Operating-point results show well-balanced discrimination and coverage (OA = 0.90, κ = 0.86), while threshold-fair analyses confirm the fusion’s advantage over other classifiers (histogram boosting, SVM-RBF, regularised QDA). LumiMaSAM supports ecological light-pollution screening by delivering spatially explicit, reliability-qualified proxy maps of ALAN heterogeneity across the surveyed scene. It fills the scale gap between satellite data and fields lighting meters (precise, sparse), providing a transferable workflow for local-scale monitoring of ALAN in structurally complex landscapes.
