A method for devising omnidirectional inelastic response models from uniaxial behavior

Authors: Henan Mao, Hüseyin Darama, Ertuǧrul Taciroğlu

Institution: Synopsys (United States), American Health Care Association, University of California, Los Angeles

Published: 2026-06-18

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

There is a wide variety of nonlinear hysteretic numerical models for simulating the mechanical responses of structural and geotechnical components or subsystems. A large subset of these models is constructed to offer only uniaxial responses and is typically packaged as zero-length macroelements. When multi-axial responses are required, these uniaxial elements are attached orthogonally to the host structural model and offer uncoupled reactions. In nonlinear loading regimes, this approach yields inaccurate estimates of both the directions and magnitudes of the reaction forces, with the latter occasionally exceeding the intrinsic capacity/strength of the modeled medium or component. A framework based on multi-surface plasticity is offered herein to address the problem. Specifically, omnidirectional macroelement models are proposed, and examples implemented in ABAQUS for “ p - y ” models used in pile-soil interaction analyses, and triple friction pendulum (TFP) bearings. The developed p - y model is verified against three-dimensional continuum finite element simulations of a laterally loaded pile embedded in sand, and the omnidirectional TFP model is validated against laboratory test data. The proposed approach can be applied to other similar problems by recalibrating the yield surfaces and hardening rules to match the uniaxial backbone curve and by adjusting the unloading-reloading rules.

Flutter of the Old Tacoma Narrows Bridge model in natural winds: Long-term experiments and statistical analysis

Authors: Qing Li, Mingjie Zhang, Fuyou Xu, P. Wang, Zhaoyu Ma, Haiyan Yu

Institution: Dalian University of Technology, Powerchina Huadong Engineering Corporation (China), Dalian Jiaotong University

Published: 2026-06-18

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

This paper presents a one-year experimental study on large-amplitude flutter responses under natural wind conditions employing a 1:8.53 full-bridge aeroelastic model of the Old Tacoma Narrows Bridge (OTNB). During this period, extensive measurements of wind fields, girder displacements, and cable tensions were conducted under non-stationary wind fields that are challenging to reproduce in conventional wind tunnel tests. A total of 137 flutter events under north wind conditions were selected to statistically analyze the wind field characteristics and structural vibration responses. The estimated flutter critical reduced wind speed ( U cr ) is generally consistent with the values reported in previous studies, which verifies the feasibility of the statistical approach employed. The results demonstrate that, in natural wind fields, both the wind parameters and their spanwise distributions significantly influence the flutter response. Specifically, increases in yaw angle and absolute value of the wind attack angle raise U cr , whereas an increase in longitudinal turbulence intensity reduces U cr . Non-uniform wind fields along the girder span can significantly reduce torsional displacement amplitudes of the girder compared with uniform wind fields of similar average wind speed and yaw angle. Moreover, during the stable stage of the selected flutter events, the maximum increment of fluctuating tension in the hangers relative to the static equilibrium value is approximately 29.5%, while that of the main cables reaches approximately 6.3%. These findings provide valuable insights for wind-resistant design and flutter stability assessment of long-span bridges.
