In-plane cyclic performance and analytical assessment of conventional mortar masonry, dry-stacked ICEB, and EMM-reinforced ICEB infill walls

Authors: Junaid Shah Khan, Sumbul Fatima, Hussain Ghazi, Azam Khan, Tatheer Zahra

Institution: National University of Sciences and Technology, Queensland University of Technology

Published: 2026-05-27

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

The seismic performance of masonry infill in reinforced concrete (RC) frames is critical for resilience in earthquake-prone regions. Although economical, unreinforced infills often fail in a brittle manner, showing signs of diagonal cracking or out-of-plane collapse. An innovative and cost-effective way to reinforce interlocking compacted earth block (ICEB) infill walls is explored in this study using expanded metal mesh (EMM). A mortar-bonded conventional infill wall, an ICEB wall that is dry-stacked, and an ICEB wall that is reinforced with EMM are the three full-scale specimens that are studied under quasi-static cyclic loading. The EMM-reinforced ICEB wall exhibited 1.56 times, 2.1 times, and 1.51 times the lateral strength, ductility, and cumulative energy dissipation of the dry-stacked ICEB wall, respectively, and 1.31 times, 2.38 times, and 1.23 times those of the mortar-bonded conventional infill wall. A strut-based analytical model is developed and calibrated using nonlinear masonry prism data to accurately predict lateral shear capacity. Its close agreement with experimental trends confirms its reliability for seismic assessment of EMM-reinforced ICEB-infilled RC frames.

Evaluation of time-varying dynamic properties of base-isolated high-rise building during lateral extension using monitored data

Authors: Daiki Sato, Yuzi Huang, Yinli Chen, Razelle Dennise A. Soriano

Institution: Building Research Institute

Published: 2026-05-27

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Other

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Understanding how building dynamics evolve during structural modifications is a critical challenge in structural engineering. Among such modifications, horizontal expansions can significantly alter the dynamic characteristics of a building. This paper addresses this challenge by presenting a case study detailing the continuous structural health monitoring of a 20-story base-isolated building during a 16-month lateral extension project. Ambient vibration data were analyzed using the Frequency Domain Decomposition method to track the time-varying changes in the building’s characteristics. The analysis yielded several key findings. A significant shift in the torsional center was observed to be closely associated with construction events, and the real-time dynamic coupling of the two linked structures was captured through a second-mode transition. The study also indicated a correlation between the variations of the dynamic properties, including natural frequency and damping ratio, and the specific construction stages. Finally, distinct evolutionary paths were noted for the translational and torsional damping ratios, suggesting their different sensitivities to physical mechanisms, as reflected by their distinct reaction timings during the construction processes.

Poro-elasto-viscoplastic Behaviour of In Situ-Heated Callovo–Oxfordian Claystone: Laboratory Investigation and Constitutive Modelling

Authors: Junfeng Ren, Philipp Braun, Carlos Plúa, Minh-Ngoc Vu, Gilles Armand, Siavash Ghabezloo

Institution: Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique, Laboratoire Navier, Université Gustave Eiffel, Agence Nationale pour la Gestion des Déchets Radioactifs

Published: 2026-05-28

Journal: Rock Mechanics and Rock Engineering

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Abstract The Callovo–Oxfordian (COx) claystone has been selected, subject to authorisation, as the potential host formation for the deep geological disposal of high-level and intermediate-level long-lived radioactive waste in France. In the repository, the COx will be exposed to thermal loads reaching up to 90 $$^{\circ }$$ <mml:math xmlns:mml="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"> <mml:mmultiscripts> <mml:mrow/> <mml:mrow/> <mml:mo>∘</mml:mo> </mml:mmultiscripts> </mml:math> C due to the decay heat of radionuclides. This study investigates the transversely isotropic poro-elasto-viscoplastic behaviour of COx claystone and evaluates the potential impact of in situ thermal loading. Rock cores previously subjected to an in situ heating experiment conducted by Andra at the Meuse/Haute–Marne Underground Research Laboratory were extracted and tested in the laboratory. Triaxial compression tests were performed under various stress paths to failure on re-saturated specimens cored either parallel or perpendicular to bedding. Cyclic loading allowed for the separation of elastic and inelastic strain components, from which stress-dependent elastic parameters were determined. The inelastic response was used to identify and calibrate a non-associated elasto-plastic constitutive model, incorporating transverse isotropy via a non-uniform scaling approach. Pore pressure dissipation following pseudo-undrained unloading enabled the estimation of intrinsic permeability. Time-dependent strain measurements confirmed the viscoplastic behaviour of COx, which was implemented in the constitutive model using an overstress formulation. The resulting poro-elasto-viscoplastic model was implemented with MFront and integrated into the finite-element code FEniCSx, enabling reproduction of the experimental results and paving the way for repository-scale simulations. Comparison between heated and non-heated samples indicates that COx claystone preserves its key properties as a suitable host rock after in situ thermal loading.
