Sequence planning and physics-informed robotic simulation for assembly of deformable beam structures

Authors: Derui Zhao, Poul Henning Kirkegaard

Institution: Aarhus University

Published: 2026-06-23

Journal: Automation in Construction

Method: Numerical Simulation

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Automated construction represents a future direction for the AEC industry, with robotic assembly playing a critical role. However, assembling beams remains challenging due to mechanical constraints and structural deformations. To narrow this gap, this paper presents an integrated framework combining sequence planning with physics-informed simulation. An enhanced genetic algorithm employs heuristic initialisation and feasibility-preserving operators to generate constructible sequences, while reducing finite element analysis calls by approximately 80% compared to a baseline without caching or early termination. The co-simulation environment integrates PyBullet for robotic motion planning with OpenSeesPy for structural analysis, enabling realistic modelling of deformation behaviour and identification of failure modes. Results demonstrate that the sequencing method produces comparable or superior sequences than the iterative constraint-tightening search method, while offering improved computational efficiency, although challenges persist for high-dimensional problems. This work narrows the gap between idealised planning and physical execution, supporting advances in the automated construction of deformable structures.

In-situ measurement method for cross-media based on multi-wavelength collaboration

Authors: Hao Zhong, Xingqiang Li, Chuanxing Liu, Rigang Cong, Jinsong Du

Institution: Shenyang Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Shenyang University of Chemical Technology

Published: 2026-06-18

Journal: Measurement

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

In the seamless integration of machining and measurement processes, the cutting fluid remaining on the workpiece surface will significantly impair the accuracy of optical in-situ measurement systems. Conventional cross-media measurement methodologies typically necessitate prior knowledge of the medium and struggle to accommodate the complex real-world measurement environments, thus hindering the attainment of precise displacement measurements. To address the aforementioned challenge, this paper proposes an innovative methodology for achieving micron-level displacement measurement on surfaces with residual cutting fluid through multi-wavelength collaboration. This innovative approach leverages the variations in optical path length induced by differences in refractive indices across multiple wavelengths to determine the measured displacement values. Based on this methodology, a measurement system is designed and constructed, and comprehensive experimental validations are conducted to verify the efficacy of the proposed method. Experimental results indicate that under diverse cross-media working conditions, the multi-wavelength collaborative measurement method proposed in this study reduces measurement errors by more than 91% compared with the traditional laser triangulation method.

Generalization assessment of data-driven hydroelastic models for flexible offshore structures across distinct sea states

Authors: Qi Zhang, Ould el Moctar, Changqing Jiang

Institution: University of Duisburg-Essen, Technische Universität Berlin

Published: 2026-06-23

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Fast and reliable prediction of hydroelastic responses is essential for the design and monitoring of flexible offshore structures, yet conventional simulation tools struggle to capture nonlinear and stochastic wave effects efficiently. This study develops a data-driven surrogate based on long short-term memory (LSTM) networks to predict hydroelastic responses of a top-fixed flexible cylinder in irregular seas. Using two sea states with similar spectral characteristics but different nonlinearity levels, we conduct a systematic, experimentally validated assessment of model generalization through within-sea-state validation and cross-sea-state transfer assessment. Results show that while the surrogate performs accurately within its training sea state, its ability to generalize depends strongly on the nonlinear characteristics of the training data. A model trained on the more nonlinear sea state transfers effectively to the milder one, whereas the reverse leads to significant underprediction of peak responses. The findings demonstrate the importance of nonlinear exposure in training datasets and provide practical insight for constructing robust, fast surrogate models for hydroelastic prediction in offshore engineering applications.
