Video-driven Gaussian splatting for as-built building geometry with energy simulation

Authors: Soumyadeep Chowdhury, Misbaudeen Aderemi Adesanya, N. Mohammed, Kanwarpartap Singh Gill, Kuljeet Singh Grewal

Institution: University of Prince Edward Island

Published: 2026-05-09

Journal: Automation in Construction

Method: Numerical Simulation

Citations: 0

Abstract:

This study introduces Gaussian-based envelope modeling automation (GEMA), a framework that uses Gaussian splatting to convert video footage into closed-surface 3D models suitable for building energy modeling (BEM). Focusing on exterior envelope geometry (LoD3), GEMA reconstructs the planar building surfaces needed for BEM. The pipeline integrates structure-from-motion, 2D Gaussian splatting, α-blending, and planar-SSIM to enhance surface accuracy. Tested residential buildings, GEMA achieved geometric deviations under ≤ 20.47%, and ASHRAE suggested hourly CV(RMSE) ≤ 30% for simulations compared to ground-truth simulations. Densification improved surface fidelity and simulation stability, with a resolution sensitivity analysis showing that annual predictions remained stable within ∼0.5–12.5% of a ground-truth baseline. A planar similarity score (P-SSIM) based on SSIM and a discrete adaptation of the 3D Radon transform is proposed to evaluate simple meshes. GEMA supports construction digital workflows by enabling automated reconstruction of as-built building envelopes from video data, producing simulation-ready geometry for energy analysis and sustainable building design. • GEMA converts drone video to BEM-ready LoD3 meshes via 2D Gaussian splatting. • P-SSIM metric evaluates mesh planarity using SSIM and 3D Radon transform. • Densification improves surface fidelity and energy prediction stability. • Scalable video-driven workflow reduces reliance on LiDAR or manual modeling.

Integrated transformer approach for shield attitude prediction in marine soft soils considering small curvature effects

Authors: Ziyao Xu, Jun Shen, Xiang Shen, Xiaohua Bao, Xiangsheng Chen, Hongzhi Cui

Institution: Shenzhen University

Published: 2026-05-08

Journal: Automation in Construction

Method: Machine Learning

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Accurately predicting shield attitude in marine soft soils remains challenging, particularly in small-curvature tunnel sections where excavation disturbs soil structure and causes nonlinear responses. Conventional data-driven methods often neglect soil properties and curvature effects, reducing reliability in complex ground conditions. This paper develops an integrated empirical mode decomposition (EMD)–principal component analysis (PCA)–Transformer framework that combines EMD for denoising, PCA for feature compression, and a Transformer encoder for sequence learning. Using field data from a shield tunnel excavated in marine soft soils, 32 parameters were analysed to quantify cross-dependencies. Incorporating marine soft soil parameters improved the prediction accuracy, reducing the RMSE of tail horizontal deviation by 46.15% and increasing R 2 from 0.85 to 0.97. Compared with five benchmarks, the proposed framework demonstrated superior accuracy and stability. The results highlight the importance of integrating soil behaviour and curvature constraints to achieve robust and transferable shield-attitude prediction for intelligent tunnelling in complex geological environments. • An EMD-PCA-Transformer framework is proposed for shield-attitude prediction. • Adding soil parameters cuts deviation error by 46% and raises accuracy to R 2 = 0.97. • The model outperforms five advances models with field settlement under 6.3 mm. • It enables robust prediction in complex geology by integrating soil and curvature effects.

A new metric to predict flicker effect in long tunnels and underground roads

Authors: A. Peña-García

Institution: Universidad de Granada

Published: 2026-05-09

Journal: Tunnelling and Underground Space Technology

Method: Analytical / Theoretical

Citations: 0

Abstract:

The flicker effect can be roughly defined as the visual perception of a change in luminance or spectral distribution with time. It can happen in a wide variety of situations but has a peculiarity in tunnels: the changes in the light pattern are not due to the source, but to the movement of the vehicles. In parallel, the new technologies and trends to build very long tunnels and underground roads (VLUR), where drivers stay longer, give a key role to the flicker effect. These circumstances, together with the serious consequences of lower performance and the severity of accidents in tunnels, require effective strategies to predict, quantify and minimize it. In this work, the characteristics and peculiarities of this effect in tunnels are analyzed and, departing from this framework, a new metric to assess the newly defined Flicker Frequency referred to HPD Overlap (FHO) and the potential flicker even before the installation is working, is developed. The methodology in this work has combined both theoretical considerations and calculation of the typical parameters for the main zones of the tunnel.
