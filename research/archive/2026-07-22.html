Soft sensing of crusher feed particle size distribution based on electrical signals and multi-feature data-driven models

Authors: Piotr Bortnowski, Maksymilian Ozdoba, Robert Król

Institution: Wrocław University of Science and Technology, AGH University of Krakow

Published: 2026-07-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Reliable on-line information on the particle size distribution (PSD) of crusher feed remains a key challenge in mineral processing, as existing solutions are dominated by vision-based systems or high-frequency vibro-acoustic measurements that are difficult to deploy in industrial environments. This study investigates whether low-frequency electrical signals recorded from a crusher drive system contain sufficient information to indirectly reconstruct the PSD of crushed material. The PSD is represented parametrically using the Rosin–Rammler distribution, and its shape and scale parameters are estimated from time-domain features extracted from current and power signals measured at sampling rates comparable to standard industrial data loggers. Laboratory experiments were conducted on a jaw crusher processing granite with controlled feed size distributions under a fixed closed-side setting. A gradient-boosting regression model with leakage-safe feature selection and leave-one-series-out validation was developed to assess generalization to unseen feed batches. The proposed model achieved high prediction accuracy for the shape parameter k ( R 2 = 0.91 ), while the scale parameter λ was predicted with lower accuracy ( R 2 = 0.82 ), reflecting its sensitivity to local material heterogeneity. Nevertheless, characteristic particle sizes derived from the predicted parameters were reconstructed with high accuracy ( R 2 = 0.99 for D 50 and R 2 = 0.94 for D 80 ).

Optimizing the geometric accuracy of data acquired through harnessing a handheld laser scanner in measurements of medieval city walls

Authors: Piotr Kędziorski, Marcin Jagoda, Jacek Katzer, Machi Zawidzki

Institution: Koszalin University of Technology, University of Warmia and Mazury in Olsztyn, Institute of Fundamental Technological Research, Łukasiewicz Research Network - Industrial Research Institute for Automation and Measurements, Polish Academy of Sciences

Published: 2026-07-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

This study develops and thoroughly evaluates a novel post-processing drift-correction method based on Shepard-type inverse-distance weighted (IDW) interpolation of displacement vectors. The algorithm maps local displacements of the entire handheld laser scanner (HLS) IR_RAW point cloud using sparse control points whose coordinates are determined with high-accuracy reference techniques (HLS in blue-light marker mode, terrestrial laser scanning (TLS), and electronic total station). The influence of control-point density (spacing 0.25–2.10 m), number of neighbors ( K = 2–10), and weighting power ( P = 1–3) was systematically analyzed on both a test brick wall and a real 14th-century city walls fragment in Koszalin, Poland. Cloud-to-cloud distance comparisons with independent TLS reference datasets demonstrated a dramatic improvement in geometric fidelity. In the test measurements, the share of points with errors below 3 mm rose from 35.5% (raw data) to 83–88% under optimal configurations (dense control grid, K = 4–8, P = 1–2). On the heritage wall, the best variants achieved over 92% of points within 3 mm. Local discontinuities were effectively suppressed, and the corrected clouds enabled reliable, millimeter-scale identification and quantification of material losses fully consistent with TLS results. The correction based on Shepard-type IDW interpolation significantly expands the applicability of affordable HLS technology for precise documentation and long-term monitoring of elongated cultural heritage structures at the scale investigated in this study, providing clear practical recommendations for control-point network design and algorithm parameter selection
