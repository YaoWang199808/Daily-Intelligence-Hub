Robotics and AI for prefabrication of steel building components: Systematic review and future roadmap

Authors: Misha Afaq, Souravik Dutta, Arash Hosseini Gourabpasi, Farzad Jalaei, Rafiq Ahmad

Institution: University of Alberta, National Research Council Canada

Published: 2026-06-18

Journal: Automation in Construction

Method: Review / Survey

Citations: 0

Abstract:

The increasing demand for precision, productivity, and safety in construction has accelerated adoption of robotics and AI in steel building components prefabrication. This systematic review synthesizes 134 peer-reviewed studies to evaluate the state of robotics, AI, and digital technologies, specifically BIM, DfMA, and digital twins, across design, manufacturing, and assembly workflows for steel prefabrication. Using a five-dimensional Scope-Mode-Advantage-Restriction-Transition (SMART) analytical framework, the review characterizes major research themes, technological distribution, and associated performance outcomes. It highlights domination of robotic welding, additive manufacturing, and vision-guided inspection, but limited multi-robot deployment and integration with digital technologies. The review identifies critical methodological and operational limitations in the state-of-the-art and formulates a comprehensive roadmap for advancing steel prefabrication through five sequential research thrusts. Future opportunities include unified BIM-to-fabrication data pipelines, generalizable AI models for adaptive process control, scalable multi-robot platforms, and integrated real-time digital twin workflows to enable an intelligent robotic steel prefabrication ecosystem.

A reproducible preprocessing and feature-engineering workflow for hydraulic transient data under controlled experimental conditions

Authors: Óscar J. Burgos-Méndez, Oscar E. Coronado-Hernández, Alex J. Garzón-Orduña, Helena M. Ramos, Modesto Pérez‐Sánchez

Institution: Universitat Politècnica de València, University of Cartagena, Instituto Politécnico de Lisboa

Published: 2026-06-16

Journal: Measurement

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

High-frequency hydraulic measurements commonly exhibit missing segments, impulsive artefacts and timing inconsistencies that distort transient signatures and hinder the reliable interpretation of pressure and flow responses. This study presents a reproducible preprocessing and feature-engineering workflow for transient hydraulic signals under controlled experimental conditions. The proposed methodology is organised into three sequential blocks: (i) temporal reconstruction and standardisation, including missing-value handling; (ii) robust anomaly detection and spike correction using moving-median-based statistics; and (iii) feature extraction in the time, statistical and frequency domains. The workflow was evaluated using 268 controlled transient scenarios from the SANDBOX GIRS platform in Colombia, comprising valve-closure, pressure-regulating-valve and hydropneumatic tank events. Validation included a benchmark comparison against a conventional moving-average baseline and a sensitivity analysis of the moving-median window length. Across representative scenarios, the proposed workflow achieved lower RMSE values, higher NSE values and substantially improved preservation of transient gradients than the baseline, while maintaining stable performance over a practical range of window lengths. The resulting cleaned and structured dataset provides a reproducible preprocessing layer for subsequent transient interpretation, anomaly screening and data-driven hydraulic monitoring applications.

Development and mechanical evaluation of stress-line inspired ribbed timber slabs with stay-in-place formwork

Authors: Hua Chai, Yiting Sun, Zhan Shu, Haipeng Hu, Philip F. Yuan

Published: 2026-06-20

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

While stress-line inspired ribbed slabs have been proposed and studied in concrete, their application in timber remains limited, hindered by the complexity associated with designing and constructing the intricate rib patterns. Current design tools and fabrication approach often fall short when applied to timber structures. To bridge this gap, this study introduces an integrated computational design and fabrication method for producing stress-line inspired ribbed timber slabs. A weighted Voronoi diagram-based algorithm is employed to generate rib patterns that align with stress-line distributions through radial and looped curves, adaptable to diverse slab geometries and boundary conditions. The resulting rib system—comprising radial and looped components—enables the bending of continuous radial laminae against looped, stay-in-place formwork, achieving mold-free fabrication. A preliminary numerical-experimental comparison of a single triangular slab unit was used to check the deformation trend of the finite element model. Numerically, under a set of idealized modelling assumptions, the stress-line slab showed improved deformation control compared with the conventional unreinforced and equal-volume thickened reference slabs. At the slab-edge measurement point, the calculated initial vertical stiffness improved more than 10 times compared with the unreinforced case. Besides, the estimated material-utilization improvement could reach 50.0%–51.3%. By integrating computational design with digital fabrication, this work provides a scalable solution for fabricating geometrically complex timber structures, offering a promising pathway toward more sustainable constructions.

Bridge influence line identification using convex programming from Bridge Weigh-in-Motion strain signals

Authors: Bence Szinyéri, Bence Kővári, István Völgyi, Dénes Kollár, Attila László Joó

Institution: Budapest University of Technology and Economics

Published: 2026-06-20

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

The influence line for a point on a structure shows how shear force or bending moment, or typical strain components, at that point changes as a unit load moves along the structure. The bridge influence line can be used in Bridge Weigh-in-Motion (BWIM) systems and Structural Health Monitoring (SHM) systems. Influence line identification from measurement data is a possible alternative to Finite Element Method-based solutions since measurement-based approaches do not need model validation, and expertise in numerical modelling. A novel convex programming-based influence line identification method is introduced in this paper. Its comparison with previous methods is also executed showing that this novel method results in 0.46% lower Over Relative Errors, while the Amplitude Relative Errors increase by 0.06% in different scenarios compared to the best state-of-the-art methods. The decrement in Overall Relative Error is even higher (0.82%) under high noise level conditions while the Amplitude Relative Error increment (0.09%) is kept low. Robustness tests are also conducted by introducing varying levels of noise to axle loads, vehicle location, and strain signals. Finally, the proposed method is also evaluated experimentally.
