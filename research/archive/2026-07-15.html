HoloAtt-UNet: an end-to-end model for real-time processing of cloud particle holograms

Authors: Yuhang Song, Wei Zeng, Lei Liu, Sijia Feng, Shuai Hu

Institution: National University of Defense Technology, China Meteorological Administration

Published: 2026-07-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Digital holographic imaging (DHI) enables high-resolution 3D characterization of cloud particles, but traditional processing methods are computationally intensive. To address this challenge, we propose HoloAtt-UNet, an end-to-end deep learning model that integrates a parallel dual-channel attention mechanism into a U-Net architecture. This design directly maps raw holograms to particle segmentation masks, thereby circumventing the computationally expensive multi-plane reconstruction stage and achieving significant efficiency gains. Validation using HOLODEC II airborne holographic data from the CSET campaign demonstrates that HoloAtt-UNet is 479-fold faster than the CPU-based conventional algorithm and approximately 60-fold faster than the GPU-accelerated conventional algorithm. For bulk microphysical parameters, including number concentration, liquid water content, and mean particle diameter, HoloAtt-UNet achieves normalized errors below 7% and correlation coefficients exceeding 0.8. For individual particles, the root mean square errors in x, y, z, and radius r are 18.06 pixels, 18.11 pixels, 6.34 mm, and 4.34 μm, respectively, with an overall missed detection rate of 11.1% and a false positive rate of 6.9%. The model also shows stable intra-campaign performance across stratiform and cumulus samples within the CSET campaign, with errors below 13% and low correlations (CC < 0.45) between prediction quality (SSIM/PSNR) and interference variables such as brightness and noise. These results indicate that HoloAtt-UNet is a promising approach for accelerated processing of airborne holographic observations within the CSET campaign, while its generalizability and real-time airborne deployment require further validation on independent datasets and target hardware.
