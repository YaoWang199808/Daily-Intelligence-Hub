A comparative assessment of open-hardware and commercial energy meters for non-intrusive load monitoring

Authors: Carlos Rodríguez-Navarro, Francisco Portillo, Manuel Soler‐Ortiz, Alfredo Alcayde

Institution: University of Almería

Published: 2026-06-15

Journal: Measurement

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

The accuracy, resolution, and spectral content of electrical measurement systems play a critical role in the reliability of energy analysis applications. This paper presents a comparative application-level assessment of open-hardware and commercial energy meters, examining how their metrological characteristics relate to performance in non-intrusive load monitoring (NILM) tasks within a common evaluation framework. Two open-hardware instruments, OpenZmeter (oZm) and Open Multi Power Meter (OMPM), are assessed against reference datasets acquired with commercial meters using identical energy disaggregation workflows implemented in NILMTK and widely adopted performance metrics. In addition to conventional electrical quantities, the effect of high-resolution harmonic measurements, available up to the 50th order in oZm, is explicitly analyzed. The results show that OMPM achieves very low Energy Assignment Error (0.006) and low Root Mean Square Error (14.782). At the same time, oZm provides application-level performance that is competitive with that of professional power-quality analyzers under the evaluated conditions. The inclusion of harmonic information increases the F1-score by 10–15% and reduces error metrics by more than 40% for loads with distinctive spectral signatures. A normalized GlobalScore, used here as an exploratory aggregate indicator, was computed for the subset of datasets with complete metric availability and revealed consistent comparative trends between measurement quality and NILM performance. Although cross-dataset differences in load composition, operating conditions, and acquisition settings limit strict one-to-one equivalence, the results support the use of open-hardware energy meters as reliable, reproducible, and cost-effective instrumentation for NILM-oriented energy monitoring and research

Phase-based spectral method for accurate impulse acoustic event localization

Authors: Jakub Svatoš, Martin Maxa, Jan Holub

Institution: Czech Technical University in Prague

Published: 2026-06-14

Journal: Measurement

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

This paper introduces a novel method for acoustic event localization, specifically targeting shooter position estimation. The approach employs a dual-microphone sensor unit and processes recorded signals in the frequency domain, using a phase-based delay estimation algorithm with custom asymmetrical windowing and amplitude-weighted delay aggregation to determine the Time Difference of Arrival and Angle of Arrival. The method was validated through experiments conducted in both an anechoic chamber and real-world shooting range conditions. The results demonstrate that the proposed approach outperforms conventional correlation-based techniques, such as Generalized Cross-Correlation and Generalized Cross-Correlation with Phase Transform, particularly in noisy environments. Even in the presence of intense ambient noise, the TDoA estimation achieves a mean error of less than 1 ms. Based on the achieved angular uncertainty of 0.9°, system-level simulations indicate a spatial localization error within ±3 m at a distance of 100 m. These results demonstrate the robustness and practical applicability of the method for impulsive acoustic events and accurate shooter localization. The algorithm is well-suited for real-time deployment in defense, law enforcement, and security applications, particularly on low-power embedded systems with strict computational and energy constraints.

Planar microwave sensor with machine learning analysis for non-destructive quantification of methanol contaminants in distilled spirits

Authors: Maria Elizabeth Teixeira Santana Praxedes, Bruno Matias de Sousa, Valdemir Praxedes da Silva Neto, Adaildo G. D'Assunção

Institution: Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Norte

Published: 2026-06-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

This paper presents a dual-port planar microwave sensor designed for the non-destructive detection and quantification of methanol contamination in distilled alcoholic beverages. The proposed sensing structure is based on a compact U-shaped microstrip line integrated with a Defected Ground Structure (DGS) that combines a Complementary Split-Ring Resonator (CSRR) and a Complementary Interdigital Capacitor (CIDC). This hybrid DGS configuration enhances the local electric field distribution and strengthens the electromagnetic coupling between the resonant elements, thereby improving the overall sensitivity of the sensor. Consequently, the device exhibits a stable and reliable response over a wide range of liquid dielectric permittivities, from 1 to 100, with an average percentage sensitivity of 1.39% and consistent, traceable resonance behavior across the entire range. Additionally, the proposed approach achieves low error in methanol concentration estimation (below 5%), confirming its accuracy and reliability. The performed measurements conducted at ambient conditions, combined with a geometrically robust design and enhanced electromagnetic field confinement, mitigate the effects of environmental fluctuations and fabrication tolerances, ensuring stable and reliable methanol detection. Machine learning methods were employed to correlate the sensor resonance frequency with the methanol concentration, using 240 real samples obtained from three types of distilled beverages at eight concentration levels. The proposed approach achieved coefficients of determination (R 2 ) exceeding 0.95, demonstrating the potential of the developed sensor and data-driven methodology for accurate and non-destructive quality assessment of distilled spirits.

Correcting time offsets and enclosure-induced measurement distortions in LiDAR–camera systems

Authors: Pasindu Ranasinghe, Dibyayan Patra, Amantha Mawathage, Bikram Pratap Banerjee, Chenxi Ye, Bingfei Nan, Simit Raval

Institution: UNSW Sydney, University of Moratuwa, University of Southern Queensland, Intelligent Health (United Kingdom)

Published: 2026-06-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Using protective enclosures for multi-sensor perception systems in hazardous environments, such as underground mines, introduces critical measurement errors that are often overlooked in sensor fusion pipelines. This paper addresses two key sources of these errors: temporal misalignment between sensor data streams and refractive distortion at the optical interface. To address temporal misalignment, an observation-based temporal calibration method is introduced, where a rotating flywheel observed by both sensors provides a direct estimate of the inter-sensor time delay, reducing the timing error from approximately 32 ms to below 3 ms without external triggering or synchronisation hardware. To account for enclosure-induced distortion, LiDAR beam refraction through a 6 mm polycarbonate dome is modelled using analytical ray tracing, validated by physics-based simulation, compared with adapted refractive baselines, and corrected in real time using a lookup-table approach with negligible computational overhead. Enclosure-aware camera intrinsic and LiDAR–camera extrinsic calibrations are then performed with the enclosure in place, treating the sensors and enclosure as a single optical system. Experimental results show that LiDAR refraction correction improves dimensional accuracy by 43% at 5 m, enclosure-aware intrinsic calibration reduces the mean reprojection error by approximately 58%, and extrinsic calibration reduces projection error from approximately 10 pixels to 2 pixels. Ablation and repeatability analyses further confirm that accurate fusion requires the combined use of LiDAR refraction correction, enclosure-aware camera calibration, and enclosure-aware extrinsic calibration.
