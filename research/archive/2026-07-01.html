Experimental investigation on prestressed concrete beams with localized strand damage: Tilt-based deflection mapping and damage indicators

Authors: Amir Shamsaddinlou, Dario De Domenico, Antonino Recupero

Institution: University of Messina

Published: 2026-06-30

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

This study investigates the structural response of prestressed concrete beams with localized strand damage under both serviceability and ultimate loading conditions. Seven simply supported beams, with a clear span of L = 7.0 m, were cast as scaled bridge girders representing different damage scenarios. Inspection windows were created at L /2, L /3, and L /4 to simulate corrosion-induced defects by selectively severing prestressing strands in a controlled manner, thereby reducing the effective prestressing steel area. The beams were tested under combined dead load and increasing midspan live load, applied through loading-unloading cycles from service levels up to failure. A dense network of MEMS tiltmeters was used to measure spanwise rotations, which were converted into deflection profiles using a straightforward slope-integration procedure that preserved support compatibility and was cross-validated against independent laser targets and LVDTs. Based on the reconstructed deflected shapes under incremental quasi-static cycles at serviceability load levels, two complementary sets of damage indicators were defined. The first set, DI₁ (loading), includes the absolute peak deflection during the load plateau (DI 1- u ) and its position (DI 1- x ). The second set, DI₂ (residual), includes the peak residual deflection after unloading (DI 2- u ) and its location (DI 2- x ). The u components reflect damage severity, while the x components provide damage localization. These indicators consistently identified the damaged regions and scaled with the number and position of severed strands, in agreement with force–displacement responses, moment–curvature relationships from sectional analysis and observed failure modes. The results demonstrate that rapid static testing combining tilt-based deflection mapping and local crack-opening measurements provides a practical and reliable approach for detecting, ranking, and localizing tendon damage in prestressed concrete girders.

Experimental shear failure of reinforced concrete beams in fire conditions

Authors: Sabine Boulvard, Duc Toan Pham, Jérémy Bleyer

Institution: Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique, Centre Scientifique et Technique du Bâtiment, Laboratoire Navier, Université Gustave Eiffel

Published: 2026-06-30

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

This paper presents the results of a test campaign of twelve reinforced concrete beams without transverse reinforcements under four-point bending and thermal loading in order to achieve their shear failure in fire conditions. Midspan deflection, temperature, shear strength and crack angle of the beams were measured throughout the campaign. Furthermore, the experimental shear strength of the beams at ambient temperature was compared to predictions from ACI Code-318–25 and EN 1992–1–1. Then, the experimental shear strength of the beams in fire conditions was compared to predictions from EN 1992–1–2 and those derived from a design model based on the upper bound kinematic approach of the yield design theory. Such comparisons were made using different values of the effectiveness factor applied to the compressive strength of concrete, available in the literature, making it possible to show the influence of such a factor on the comparative results.

Wind–vehicle–bridge interaction and driving safety reliability under skew winds

Authors: Alfredo Cámara, Chetan Jagadeesh, S. Divall

Institution: Universidad Politécnica de Madrid, Northampton Community College, City, University of London

Published: 2026-06-29

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Numerical Simulation

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Wind-induced vehicle accidents on bridges depend not only on mean wind speed but also on turbulence and pavement roughness. Existing wind–vehicle–bridge interaction (W-VBI) studies typically rely on a limited number of stochastic wind and pavement realisations, restricting the quantification of record-to-record variability in driving safety. This study proposes a W-VBI framework that explicitly embeds this variability into the driving safety assessment. Applied to a twin box-girder bridge, the methodology combines stochastic simulations of turbulent wind and pavement profiles with aerodynamic coefficients obtained from wind-tunnel tests, which are used to define wind loads in the coupled W-VBI analysis. Over 85,000 nonlinear simulations are conducted for 11 wind angles, five gust levels, two high-sided vehicles, and speeds from 15–130 km/h. Vehicle overturning, side-slipping, and yawing probabilities are calculated, using bootstrapping techniques to evaluate the statistical stability of the simulations. Results show that wind and pavement randomness induce significant record-to-record variability in vehicle safety, while bootstrapping enables estimation of the minimum number of stochastic records required for statistically stable reliability assessments.
