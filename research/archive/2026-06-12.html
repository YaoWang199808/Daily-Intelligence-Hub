Intelligent railway component inspection during construction and maintenance via YOLO-GNN architecture

Authors: Youzhi Tang, Yu Qian

Institution: University of South Carolina

Published: 2026-06-11

Journal: Automation in Construction

Method: Machine Learning

Citations: 0

Abstract:

The safety and reliability of railway systems depend on the integrity of track components, a challenge exacerbated by the heterogeneity and aging of legacy U.S. infrastructure. Ensuring component quality during construction and maintenance requires precise, context-aware inspection. While deep learning has enabled automated visual inspection, most models cannot differentiate genuinely missing components from intentionally unused installation points, a task demanding structural and spatial reasoning. This paper presents YOLO-GNN (YG-Net), a hybrid framework that integrates YOLO11-seg for rail component detection and segmentation with a graph neural network module, EGATFormer, for spatial-context-aware inference of missing spikes. EGATFormer captures baseplate-level relationships through edge-guided attention masking. To support benchmarking, the Rail Components Dataset (RCD) is introduced, containing 2100 real-world images and over 41,000 annotated components. YG-Net achieves 55.90% mAP, 50.99% Seg-mAP, and 14.76 ms/image inference, while EGATFormer improves missing-spike classification F1-score from 80.59% to 89.21%, approaching the 95.11% upper bound.

Machine learning-based residual strength prediction for quasi-statically damaged aluminum honeycomb sandwich structures

Authors: Madni Shifa, Zaigham Saeed Toor, Afsar Husain, Sanan H. Khan

Institution: Al Ain University, United Arab Emirates University, Pohang University of Science and Technology

Published: 2026-06-11

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Aluminum honeycomb sandwich panels serve as primary load-bearing members in aerospace and satellite structures, but their susceptibility to localised damage from tool drops, ground handling, and runway debris remains a recurring concern for damage-tolerance assessment. Most published compression-after-impact studies address single-impact damage; the influence of multiple, spatially distributed dents on the residual edgewise compressive strength has received limited attention. In the present work, AA6061-T6/AA5056 honeycomb panels with 1–4 hemispherical indents (viz. 5, 10, and 15 mm diameter at 30 mm centre-to-centre spacing) were tested under quasi-static indentation followed by edgewise compression after indentation (CAI). A cohesive-zone finite-element model reproduced the experimental load–displacement response to within 4.7% mean peak-load error, and a six-feature physical descriptor set was used to train Ridge and Gaussian-process surrogates which interpolate within the tested envelope. The undamaged CAI strength was 329 MPa; specimens damaged with 5 mm indenters retained over 91% of this baseline regardless of the number of dents, while four 15 mm indents reduced the strength by 29.5%. A change in degradation rate was identified at approximately 17 J of total absorbed energy, separating a regime which retained over 85% of baseline from one governed by debond-driven face-sheet buckling. Under grouped leave-one-out cross-validation, in which both the held-out experimental sample and its finite-element twin are excluded from training, Ridge regression predicted CAI strength with R 2 = 0 . 92 and RMSE = 7.2 MPa; a simple lookup baseline collapsed from R 2 = 0 . 99 to R 2 = − 0 . 17 between the two protocols, owing to the FEA-twin leak which inflates the originally reported scores. The validated surrogate enables rapid CAI-strength estimation within the tested envelope of 5–15 mm indenter diameter, 1–4 indents per panel, 30 mm centre-to-centre spacing, and quasi-static loading at room temperature.

Stability and load capacity of cylindrical tensegrity modules considering geometrical and material nonlinear behavior

Authors: M.V.B. Santana, J. Marthelot, Paulo B. Gonçalves

Institution: Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique, Aix-Marseille Université, Pontifícia Universidade Católica do Rio de Janeiro

Published: 2026-06-10

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Numerical Simulation

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Cylindrical tensegrity modules are lightweight self-stabilizing systems that derive their structural integrity from a balance of tensile forces in cables and compressive forces in struts. This study investigates their stability and load capacity, accounting for geometric and material nonlinearity. A nonlinear finite element model is developed to conduct a detailed parametric nonlinear analysis considering large displacements and rotations and the possible asymmetric response of the system. The deformation kinematics under vertical loading, which identifies a characteristic screw-like motion that critically influences its stiffness, post-buckling stability and load capacity. The proposed framework enables a comprehensive quantification of how topological, geometric, and material parameters govern the system’s ultimate load capacity and stability, thereby providing clear guidelines for the optimal design and reliable application of cylindrical tensegrity modules.

A good governance framework for strengthening the enhanced, more sustainable use of underground resources in Europe

Authors: Tamás Hámor, C. D. F. Rogers, János Kovács, Mária Hámor-Vidó

Institution: University of Pecs, University of Birmingham

Published: 2026-06-10

Journal: Tunnelling and Underground Space Technology

Method: Review / Survey

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Mapping of physical, environmental and legal conflicts and interlinkages between underground resources, excluding the surface environmental components and geological hazards, indicates that conflicts of all types occur. Groundwater, minerals and urban underground infrastructure have the most numerous conflicts, resulting from the provisions of sectoral governance (legislation, regulations, codes and standards) that are not harmonized and unfavorable physical and chemical interactions due to inappropriate planning and design. This study provides a review of the relevant European Union laws and calls for smart policy and legislation to overcome these problems. Acknowledging that many aspects remain with the sovereign jurisdictions of Member States, joint actions may govern the sector towards sustainable solutions and undistorted competition. Ownership of resources and permitting is of paramount importance and a legal subject to be resolved. The depth limitation of landowners’ rights should be clearly defined in national laws, civil codes or construction acts, because of deep garages, cellars and heat pump boreholes reaching the depth interval of other public or company resources. As a first foundational contribution, this study presents a holistic classification of underground resources, including urban underground space, and subclasses for spatial development planning and asset management. It proposes a pragmatic evaluation tool by applying the criticality assessment methodology in two alternative manners: on the economic and the resilience nexus. Fine-tuning the legislation, the transformation of land use planning into 4D spatial development planning supported by openly accessible online geoinformation system, the comparative criticality assessment, and the systematic use of strategic impact assessment for relevant national and regional programs comprise the set of potential tools and measures that can promote the enhanced, and more sustainable, use of underground space and its natural resources.

Rock Mechanics and Rock Engineering

CRFPA: A Novel Rock Fracture Process Analysis Method Coupled with Cohesive Zone Model

Authors: Jiang Yu, Bin Gong, Tingting Wang, Chun’an Tang

Institution: Northeastern University, University of London, Chang'an University, Brunel University of London, Dalian University of Technology

Published: 2026-06-11

Journal: Rock Mechanics and Rock Engineering

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Abstract To enhance the effectiveness of fracture process simulation within the Rock Fracture Process Analysis (RFPA) framework, this study proposes a cohesive-enhanced RFPA (CRFPA) method. Cohesive elements are pre-inserted along potential fracture interfaces, either globally or locally, providing modeling flexibility tailored to different simulation objectives. A dual constitutive strategy is employed: cohesive elements adopt both brittle and quasi-brittle constitutive models to accommodate a wider range of material behaviors while solid elements retain the brittle damage model of conventional RFPA. This allows accurate distinction between matrix cracking and interfacial degradation. The method is formulated within a variational framework that ensures thermodynamic consistency, guaranteeing proper energy dissipation and irreversible damage evolution throughout the entire loading history. Leveraging RFPA’s full-step iteration scheme, CRFPA achieves stable convergence while capturing progressive interface and matrix failure. Three benchmark examples, a masonry wallette shear test, a Brazilian disc splitting test, and flaw-driven crack propagation, are used to validate CRFPA method. Simulation results show strong agreement with experimental and analytical references, highlighting improvements in crack path realism, stress field resolution, and failure mode prediction compared to traditional RFPA. This study establishes CRFPA as a robust and versatile numerical framework for multi-scale fracture analysis in quasi-brittle materials.

An Elasto-Damage Constitutive Model with Nonlinear Failure Criterion for Quasi-Brittle Intact Rocks

Authors: Vahid Azamipour, Hamid Roshan, Rafael Jimenez, Hossein Masoumi

Institution: Monash University, UNSW Sydney, Universidad Politécnica de Madrid

Published: 2026-06-11

Journal: Rock Mechanics and Rock Engineering

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Abstract Intact rocks exhibit complex stress–strain behaviour that is controlled by its intrinsic characteristics such as mineralogical composition, damage evolution and the level of brittleness as well as the external factors including loading conditions. Conventional constitutive models often fail to predict such complex responses due to their lack of integration of intrinsic and external factors or shortcomings to capture the transition from brittle to ductile response and strength degradation. This paper introduces a novel elasto-damage constitutive framework for capturing the damage behaviour of quasi-brittle intact rocks across a range of loading conditions. The proposed model employs a nonlinear failure criterion and integrates it with a bounding surface framework and damage evolution, introducing a novel inelastic damage hardening concept, where the evolution of damage bounding surface is governed by a coupled influence of inelastic volumetric strain and inelastic shear strain, allowing a more accurate representation of stiffness degradation and strength reduction. Additionally, the radial mapping rule is employed to capture the interplay between shear and volumetric strains, improving predictions under various loading conditions. The model is validated against the laboratory data obtained from Vosges and Darley Dale sandstones under a range of confining pressures, exhibiting an acceptable agreement between the model predictions and the experimental results. The analyses also highlight the model's precision in simulating the complex nonlinear mechanical characteristics of quasi-brittle intact rocks applicable to rock engineering projects within civil, mining and geo-energy disciplines.
