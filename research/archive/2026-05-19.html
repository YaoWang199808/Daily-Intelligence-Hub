Design and development of a windscreen wiper motor durability test system

Authors: xiaokun Hu, junlu Cheng, Guangzhao Tian, Wei Qiu

Institution: Nanjing Agricultural University

Published: 2026-05-16

Journal: Measurement

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

• Proposed an integrated wiper motor durability test system covering multi-parameter measurement and condition simulation. • Achieved 1% accuracy calibration of torque sensors via attitude correction and polynomial fitting. • Established a dynamic mapping model for magnetic powder brakes to simulate real motor loads with high precision. In response to the need for high-fidelity durability evaluation of automotive wiper motors, this paper develops an integrated test system that combines synchronized acquisition of torque, voltage, current, rotational speed, and temperature with in-situ calibration and programmable load simulation. Beyond conventional bench integration, the proposed method introduces an attitude-corrected torque-sensor calibration scheme based on a measuring arm and pose sensor, a residual-based outlier screening and polynomial-model-selection workflow, and a calibrated current-torque mapping for magnetic powder brakes that enables closed-loop reproduction of real wiper loads. To reduce inter-station discrepancies, torque-sensor calibration is evaluated with weighted residual sum of squares and root mean square error, through which a quadratic model is selected as the best compromise between accuracy and robustness; for the magnetic powder brake, a cubic model captures the weakly nonlinear current-torque relationship under dynamic operation. Representative cyclic endurance-operation results over approximately seven successive wiping periods show stable and repeatable corrected-torque and brake-current waveforms across successive wiping cycles, while the selected torque-calibration model satisfies the full-scale accuracy requirement. A brief uncertainty analysis based on the static torque-reference chain and the dynamic brake-control chain indicates that the dominant error sources arise from sensor sensitivity, arm-length/angle calibration, and fitting residuals rather than from the acquisition-module resolution. The proposed system therefore provides a reliable platform for durability evaluation, control optimization, and life assessment of automotive wiper motors.

Experimental investigation of a dual-spar floating offshore wind farm with a shared mooring line: Extended campaign with multiple wave conditions, improved instrumentation, and reduced mooring stiffness

Authors: Daniel Ponce-Matilla, Antonio Medina-Mánuel, Amalia Castillo-Martinez, Connor Sterling, Julio Oria-Escudero, Leandro Saavedra Ynocente, Adolfo Marón, Sergio Amores-Carballo, Tomas Lopez-Olocco, Javier Moreno-Robles

Institution: Canal de Experiencias Hidrodinámicas de El Pardo (Spain), Universidad Politécnica de Madrid, American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Ta Solutions (China), European Telecommunications Standards Institute

Published: 2026-05-18

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Shared mooring offers potential cost benefits to floating wind farms. However, it also introduces complex dynamic interactions between multiple floating structures and an interconnected mooring subsystem. From a scientific perspective, shared-mooring floating offshore wind turbines constitute strongly coupled multi-physics systems, where aero-hydrodynamic loading, mooring-line nonlinearities, and platform rigid-body dynamics interact across multiple time and length scales. Experimental investigation of such systems remains particularly challenging due to (a) the large number of variables that must be monitored over widespread areas in the laboratory, (b) the difficulty of setting up target configurations and pretensioning models appropriately, and (c) the complexity required to introduce external forcing in the form of waves, wind, and current. This paper presents an experimental study on a dual-spar floating offshore wind farm as a continuation and complement to Lopez-Olocco et al. (2023) and Liang et al. (2024). In the present paper, substantial improvements have been introduced in the experimental setup. Specifically, the use of pulleys to transmit mooring line tension has been eliminated, the tension is now measured at all fairleads, and the motions are measured on both floaters. In addition, the stiffness of the dual-spar system has been reduced by changing the characteristics of the shared line. Finally, the test matrix has been extended to include decay tests exploring coupled surge and sway modes, as well as bichromatic waves. The present and previous configurations are still comparable, but differences are large enough that they require distinct numerical modeling. In fact, the dramatic snap-slack events in the shared line found by Lopez-Olocco et al. (2023) are not found under similar wave conditions in the present reduced stiffness configuration. Also, first-order wave frequency peaks and near-zero troughs in the shared line tension PSDs have been explained here by the dynamics of relative spar motion. Overall, the results presented aim to enhance the understanding of the floaters and mooring line dynamics resulting from shared line configurations while providing benchmark data for researchers and industry. To this latter aim, multimedia of the experiments, MATLAB format figure files with all the curves in the paper, and the OrcaFlex model file with the static setup, are made available in the link https://short.upm.es/ixe17 for general use. • Scarce experimental research on shared mooring lines for floating wind turbines. • Large improvements introduced in the experimental setup wrt previous literature. • Shared line with reduced stiffness wrt previous experimental literature tested. • Dramatic snap-slack events in shared line in previous literature not existing here. • Results suggest that shared mooring can be a practical solution for cost effective mooring.
