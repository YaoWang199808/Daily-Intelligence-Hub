Automation in Construction

Trust as a mediator between physical climate conditions and human–robot collaboration efficiency in construction

Authors: Jie Zhang, Kaiqiang Wang, Yi Yang, Yue Wang, Changyong Liu, Kailun Feng

Institution: Harbin Institute of Technology, Heilongjiang Institute of Technology, Umeå University

Published: 2026-07-18

Journal: Automation in Construction

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Human–robot collaborative performance is influenced by physical workplace conditions, which can affect performance indirectly through workers' physiological comfort, cognitive load, and situational awareness. Under summer high-temperature conditions, construction sites impose increased environmental constraints on collaboration. This paper investigates how adverse summer working environments influence human–robot collaboration efficiency, and whether workers' trust in robots mediates this relationship. A pilot experiment was conducted using a representative rebar-tying task to compare human–robot collaboration in two construction environments: a relatively comfortable, factory-like environment enabled by an aerial building machine (ABM) and a conventional non-ABM environment characterized by more adverse working conditions. Results show that the ABM environment improves collaborative efficiency by approximately 19.8% and enhances trust. Trust also significantly mediates the effect of environment on performance. These findings offer insights into human–robot collaboration and guide intelligent construction under high-temperature conditions.

A generalization of the Whittaker–Shannon interpolation formula for non-bandlimited signals

Authors: Tamás Haba, Zsanett Bertók, Csaba Budai

Institution: Budapest University of Technology and Economics

Published: 2026-07-18

Journal: Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Reconstructing continuous signals from discrete samples is a fundamental task in interpolation theory. Under ideal assumptions, the classical Whittaker–Shannon interpolation formula guarantees perfect reconstruction for bandlimited signals sampled at the Nyquist rate. However, many real-world systems, particularly sampled-data systems, produce non-bandlimited signals whose intersample behavior is governed by the continuous-time dynamics encoded in the system model, making classical bandlimited reconstruction methods inappropriate. This paper presents a generalized interpolation framework that reconstructs continuous-time outputs from their samples using a model-induced kernel, thereby extending Shannon’s formula to a broader class of signals. The proposed method derives the interpolation kernel from the governing equations of the underlying system, enabling accurate reconstruction of intersample content. Numerical simulations and laboratory experiments on a permanent magnet synchronous machine (PMSM) validate the approach, demonstrating its effectiveness in reconstructing intersample behavior from output samples by leveraging model information.

Experimentally validated reconfigurable piezoelectric metamaterial plate for sound directivity control

Authors: Camila Sanches Schimidt, Stefan Janssen, Leopoldo Pisanelli Rodrigues de Oliveira, Elke Deckers, Claus Claeys, Carlos De Marqui

Institution: Universidade de São Paulo, Flanders Make (Belgium), Hasselt University, KU Leuven

Published: 2026-06-19

Journal: Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

This work presents an experimentally validated investigation of a reconfigurable piezoelectric metamaterial plate designed for sound directivity control. The metastructure consists of an aluminum plate with an array of piezoelectric patches, each connected to a tunable resonant shunt circuit. By selectively adjusting the spatial distribution of tuned and open-circuit elements, it becomes possible to modify vibration modes and steer the radiated acoustic energy without physically modifying the structure. The study presents transverse velocity and near-field acoustic measurements of the metastructure, which also validate the finite element simulations of the vibroacoustic system. Results demonstrate that different tuning patterns can confine vibrations to specific regions of the plate, producing far-field radiation patterns that range from omnidirectional to highly directional lobes. These findings emphasize the potential of reconfigurable piezoelectric metamaterials as a versatile solution for adaptive noise and vibration control in engineering applications.

Engineering Structures

Coupled bending-shear-torsion behaviors of U-shaped composite girders with corrugated steel webs

Authors: Shenggang Chen, S Y Li, Zhou Yu, Yang zhi, J Zhang, Lin Xiaoshan

Institution: China University of Mining and Technology, Yunnan Machinery Research and Design Institute, Yunnan Investment Group (China), China State Construction Engineering (China), RMIT University

Published: 2026-07-18

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

U-shaped composite girders with corrugated steel webs (U-CSW) exhibit considerable promise for widespread implementation in urban rail transit viaducts, owing to their superior prestressing efficiency, excellent shear resistance and effective mitigation of cracking. During service, eccentric loading subjects U-CSW members to coupled bending–shear–torsion interactions, yet the underlying interaction mechanisms remain insufficiently understood. This study systematically investigates the mechanical behavior and failure mechanisms of U-CSW under combined bending–shear–torsion loading, and proposes a predictive approach for determining the ultimate bearing capacity associated with distinct failure modes. Finite element models of U-CSW are developed and validated against available experimental data, followed by comprehensive parametric analyses incorporating key parameters such as the bending-to-torsion ratio, web thickness-to-height ratio, span-to-height ratio, width-to-height ratio, and boundary conditions. The findings indicate that the dominant failure mode of U-CSW shifts from flexural failure to bottom slab shear failure as the bending-to-torsion ratio decreases. Simply supported U-CSW with shorter spans are particularly susceptible to bottom slab shear failure, whereas smaller web thickness-to-height ratios (e.g., 1:250, 1:167, 1:147) promote shear buckling failure in the corrugated steel plates. Through theoretical derivation and numerical simulations, interaction relationships among bending, shear, and torsion are established. These research outcomes offer valuable theoretical support and practical design guidance for the application of U-CSW under eccentric loading scenarios.

Fatigue behavior and life prediction of frost-damaged reinforced concrete beams under water submersion: Experimental and numerical study

Authors: Yanyue Qin, Kai Matsutani, Kazunori Miyanaga, Hideto Kida, Xi Ji, Yuya Takahashi

Institution: The University of Tokyo, Tsumura Research Institute (Japan)

Published: 2026-07-18

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Reinforced concrete (RC) bridge deck slabs in cold regions are frequently subjected to both fatigue loading and freeze–thaw cycles (FTC), leading to progressive deterioration. However, current design approaches typically treat these mechanisms independently, which may result in non-conservative service life predictions. To elucidate the influence of FTC on fatigue performance, three-point bending fatigue tests were conducted on RC beams under dry, submerged, and combined FTC and submersion conditions. Under severe frost damage, fatigue life is reduced by 80% relative to wet fatigue and by up to 98.3% relative to dry fatigue. A finite element model was developed and calibrated within a multi-scale computational framework to quantitatively analyze the fatigue behavior of frost-damaged RC beams. Based on numerical and experimental results, an empirical prediction formula was proposed for the reduction factor for wet fatigue life ( R F ) from the relative dynamic modulus of elasticity (RDME).
