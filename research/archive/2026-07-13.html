Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing

Operational modal analysis of a rotating blade using continuously scanning laser Doppler vibrometer measurements

Authors: Li Dong, Daniel J. Rixen

Institution: Technical University of Munich

Published: 2026-06-08

Journal: Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Operational modal analysis (OMA) is a powerful technique for identifying the dynamic characteristics of rotating blades under operating conditions. One limitation of standard OMA approaches, however, is that linear time invariance is assumed, which is not the case for wind turbines with rotating blades. Traditional vibration measurement techniques face limitations due to the challenges of capturing high-resolution modal data on rotating blades and possibly not detecting problems in all parts of the blade. This paper presents an extended harmonic power spectrum method for OMA using a continuously tracking and scanning laser Doppler vibrometer (CTSLDV) system, which enables measuring with a wind turbine in operation. A test rig with a small wind turbine is used in this work to evaluate the proposed measurement and analysis methods. The CTSLDV system continuously scans on the central axis of the blade under operational conditions and the mode shapes are successfully estimated using different scan frequencies. The results highlight the advantages of using a continuous scanning and extended harmonic power spectrum method for full-field vibration analysis of the linear time-periodic systems, which can enhance structural health monitoring and predictive maintenance of rotating machinery under operational conditions.

Engineering perspectives on spin-induced wave steering in elastic media

Authors: Luiz Henrique Marra da Silva Ribeiro, José Roberto de França Arruda, Carlos De Marqui

Institution: Universidade de São Paulo, Universidade Estadual de Campinas (UNICAMP), Hospital Universitário da Universidade de São Paulo

Published: 2026-05-22

Journal: Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Spin phenomena are well known in quantum mechanics, but recent works have shown that spin can also be found in continuum mechanics. Spin angular momentum (SAM) can be a mechanism for steering elastic waves through phase-shifted external excitations. While spin-related phenomena have been mainly addressed in physics-oriented studies, an engineering perspective supported by analytical tools remains lacking. It has been demonstrated in previous studies that combined shear and bending excitation with suitable magnitude and phase can induce one-sided propagation. In this work, we interpret the wave steering phenomenon in beams from an engineering perspective using a frequency-domain wave propagation solution. Furthermore, we derive analytical expressions for the magnitude and phase relation of the shear force and bending moment that cause SAM in Euler-Bernoulli beams. These analytical expressions are dependent on the excitation frequency and wavenumber. It is shown that the required phase angle for optimal unidirectional propagation is 90 degrees. Spectral and time-domain numerical simulations and experiments confirm that switching the sign of the SAM changes the propagation direction, and that the ratio between the magnitudes of shear and bending moment for optimal wave steering varies with frequency. These results establish a consistent engineering perspective on spin-driven wave control and pave the way for practical applications.

A low-speed camera DIC approach for measuring synchronous vibrations in rotating disks

Authors: Serena Occhipinti, Alessandra Cesaretti, Christian Maria Firrone, Paolo Neri, Daniele Botto

Institution: Politecnico di Torino

Published: 2026-05-04

Journal: Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

During operation, turbomachinery blades are subjected to dynamic excitations, which are the primary drivers of high-cycle fatigue, the leading cause of failure in bladed disks. Therefore, accurate measurement of their dynamic response under resonance is critical for reliable design. Traditional contact-based measurement techniques suffer from drawbacks such as added mass, limited lifespan, and complex data transmission, prompting interest in non-contact alternatives. This paper explores the application of digital image correlation (DIC) for analyzing synchronous vibrations in bladed disks. The proposed approach employs low-speed, high-resolution cameras combined with downsampling strategies to enable cost-effective, full-field, high-resolution measurements. A signal processing technique, the circumferential harmonic fit (CHF), is proposed and applied to extract system parameters from downsampled displacement data. The methodology is applied on a simplified bladed disk mounted on a spinning rig, demonstrating successful identification of operational deflection shapes at resonance. • Dynamics of rotating disks recorded at a sampling rate below the Nyquist limit. • Circumferential harmonic adaptation fit was used to avoid aliasing errors. • Excitation of the dummy disk using permanent magnets.
