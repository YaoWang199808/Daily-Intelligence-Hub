Robustness evaluation of phase-based displacement measurement under illumination variation conditions

Authors: Sida Ai, Zhenkun Li

Institution: University of Alberta, Aalto University

Published: 2026-07-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Vision-based structural displacement measurement has attracted increasing attention in structural health monitoring because of its non-contact nature, low cost, and high spatial resolution. However, the performance of many conventional intensity-based methods is highly sensitive to illumination conditions, which limits their long-term application in real engineering environments. Although phase-based methods have been suggested to be more robust to illumination variations, this view has largely been based on theoretical inference in previous studies, and systematic experimental validation under representative lighting conditions remains limited. Therefore, in this study, controlled laboratory experiments were conducted under different illumination intensities, time-varying illumination, and fog-induced dynamic spatial interference to investigate the performance of the phase-based method for vision-based structural displacement measurement. A laser displacement sensor was used as the reference measurement. For comparison, two additional optical flow methods, namely intensity-based optical flow and deep learning-based optical flow, were also implemented. The results show that, under the tested low-light and time-varying illumination conditions in laboratory settings, the phase-based method provides more robust displacement measurement, with better accuracy and signal stability, than the other two methods.
