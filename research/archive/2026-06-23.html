GRAM: Graph-Guided Robust Registration with multi-scale compatibility for point clouds

Authors: Hao Tan, Zetao Lian, Jie Yang, Yue Wang

Institution: Anhui University of Science and Technology, Nanjing Surveying and Mapping Research Institute (China), Wuhu Institute of Technology

Published: 2026-06-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Point cloud registration remains difficult in spatial metrology and large-scale 3D sensing because cross-source data fusion suffers from extreme outlier ratios ( > 99 % ), heavy sensor noise, and unknown scale drift. Algorithms using a single, fixed distance threshold generally fail to separate true inliers from structured noise. To solve this, we propose GRAM: a Graph-Guided Robust Registration framework with Multi-Scale Compatibility. In its front-end, GRAM builds a multi-scale Second-Order Spatial Compatibility ( SC 2 ) pyramid to isolate true inliers from pseudo-structured outliers, using graph-derived confidence scores to form higher-order spatial hypotheses. To manage cross-source scale drift, the back-end uses a lightweight scale-consistency discriminator that automatically switches between two states: a hard truncation mode for rigid SE ( 3 ) precision, and a progressive annealing mode for Sim ( 3 ) scale compensation. We tested GRAM across standard datasets, including long-term mobile mapping (KITTI) and large-scale topographic measurement (WHU-TLS). The results show it provides a reliable, fast, and practical registration solution for field environments with extreme outliers.

Smartphone-based photogrammetry for synthetic surface crack detection in reinforced concrete bridge structures

Authors: Sharon Fajemiyo, Ekin Özer

Institution: University College Dublin

Published: 2026-06-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Surface cracking in reinforced concrete bridges is a critical indicator of structural deterioration. Recent studies merge computer vision and point cloud data for crack detection but require scientific-grade instrumentation and aerial data. This study investigates a citizen science approach: smartphone-based photogrammetry as a low-cost solution for capturing and interpreting such damage under real lighting conditions. Smartphone photogrammetric models were created and analysed through visual inspection, RGB colour variance, point cloud alignment via Iterative Closest Point (ICP), and lightweight image-based detection algorithms, including thresholding, subtraction, structural similarity index measure (SSIM), and edge detection. Results show that, with optimised image capture and prioritising lighting, angle diversity, and overlap, synthetic surface cracks simulating shear and flexural failures are detectable. While RGB variance shows diagnostic limitations due to environmental sensitivity, ICP-enabled spatial coherence proved crucial for comparative analysis. While direct thresholding yielded more interpretable features in a qualitative setting, it is observed that quantitative algorithmic performance remains mixed depending on the evaluation metric. Notable examples include direct thresholding with better precision findings, whereas SSIM and edge detection indicate better recall rates. With a structured workflow, consumer-grade devices offer a scalable alternative to traditional reconnaissance methods.

Experimental dynamic out-of-plane response of masonry buildings: mechanism activation, progression, and collapse

Authors: Dario Vecchio, Babar Ilyas, Nuno Mendes, Paulo B. Lourenço

Institution: University of Minho

Published: 2026-06-18

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Many uncertainties are related to the seismic out-of-plane (OOP) response of unreinforced masonry (URM) structures. Challenges include the prediction of the triggering acceleration, velocity, and their role on the final collapse mechanism. To date, shake-table tests have investigated limited boundary condition scenarios, and the common unrestrained wall-to-wall and wall-to-diaphragm connections lack investigation. These conditions lead to hybrid two-way bending, a response featuring simultaneous rocking and horizontal bending, so far observed only in a static environment. This paper discusses an extensive shake-table campaign comprising 211 tests to observe the OOP response of URM specimens with no top horizontal connection subjected to structural and input variations. A novel methodology combining dynamic testing and Digital Image Correlation (DIC) techniques is proposed. Mechanism interaction is addressed in three instances, namely activation, progression, and near-collapse. Results demonstrate the existence of hybrid two-way bending, characterised by sustained horizontal one-way bending cycles, and short, sudden rocking events. Five distinct collapse mechanisms are observed, thereby strengthening the literature on the issue. The campaign has generated a robust experimental dataset, which supports validation of analytical and numerical models, as well as further probabilistic analyses (i.e., fragility analysis).
