An Integrated novel random decrement technique and system identification framework for modal features extraction in structural health monitoring

Authors: Cristian Puerto-Santana, Javier Diaz-Rozo, Carlos Ocampo-Martinez

Institution: Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya

Published: 2026-07-15

Journal: Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Structural health monitoring (SHM) techniques are used for monitoring the condition of structures, including bridges, buildings, dams, among others, through data acquired from embedded sensors. An integral aspect of SHM involves feature extraction, wherein algorithms analyze raw sensor data to generate explainable features regarding the health state of structures. The random decrement technique (RDT) is a notable signal processing method used to estimate the free response of mechanical structures, crucial for extracting frequency features related to structural dynamics. However, studies have shown that RDT encounters performance issues in cases of nonlinear damping, significant signal noise, and overlapping vibration modes. In a previous study, a data-driven classifier for transient and stationary regimes in multivariate dynamic systems was introduced, employing differential geometry tools to analyze stochastic properties. In this paper, the authors applied this classifier to develop a novel frequency-feature extraction method for SHM. This method incorporates a modified triggering condition for RDT and a recent system identification technique, aiming to improve frequency-feature estimation, especially in segment selection and modal parameter estimation of free responses in non-linear structures. Validation of the proposed approach employed mechanical vibration data from four accelerometer sensors on the “Andoian DF 12” bridge in Guipuzkoa, Spain, covering a one-year period and providing comprehensive insights into structural dynamics across different seasons. Comparative analysis against traditional RDT and various system identification techniques demonstrated that the proposed methodology improve the estimation of structural free responses by reducing noise and requiring fewer data compared to traditional RDTs. Moreover, the estimated modes aligned with the spectral content of mechanical sensors, indicating suitability for online applications with minimal computational costs and accurate modal parameter estimation for SHM in structures.

Comparative analysis of shearlet and wavelet transforms for vibro-acoustography image enhancement

Authors: Bruno Silva Marció, Artur Antonio Seibert, Rodolfo C.C. Flesch

Institution: Universidade Federal de Mato Grosso, Universidade Federal de Santa Catarina

Published: 2026-07-13

Journal: Measurement

Method: Experimental

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Despite being a powerful nondestructive inspection technique, vibro-acoustography (VA) suffers from image quality issues caused by intense wave reverberation artifacts. This phenomenon manifests as bright and dark fringes, resulting in a highly anisotropic and heterogeneous image background. This paper proposes a shearlet transform-based method designed to mitigate these intense fringes while preserving defect characteristics in VA images. Experimental tests were conducted on a metal sample with machined circular defects that presented different sizes and shapes. For comparison purposes, the images generated by the proposed shearlet method were evaluated with those processed using a two-level fourth-order Daubechies wavelet approach. Both qualitative and quantitative analyses were conducted to evaluate their performance, and the results confirmed that both shearlet and wavelet methods significantly reduced reverberation artifacts in the original VA images. Regarding the qualitative analysis, while the wavelet approach achieved considerable artifact reduction, resulting in images with a more uniform background, it also compromised the characterization of the defects. In contrast, the shearlet method provided superior performance in preserving the contours of the machined defects, despite its less intense artifact attenuation. Further quantitative assessment supported these observations. The wavelet method demonstrated superior artifact attenuation and background uniformity, reducing the standard deviation of pixel intensities in background areas from the original VA image by up to approximately 50%. However, for defect feature preservation, the shearlet method demonstrated significantly higher peak signal-to-noise ratio values, (e.g., reaching approximately double the values of the wavelet approach), thus proving more robust in maintaining the integrity of defect characteristics.

Optimisation of ultrasonic array inspection of highly scattering materials

Authors: Alexander Velichko

Institution: University of Bristol

Published: 2026-07-01

Journal: Ultrasonics

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Optimization of ultrasonic array imaging performance for efficient defect detection in polycrystalline materials is an important practical problem that has gained significant attention. Although the primary physical factors affecting the propagation of ultrasonic waves in highly scattering materials are generally well understood, existing ultrasonic inspection optimisation approaches are mainly driven by empirical qualitative rules. The conventional quantitative measure for defect detectability is the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), which is defined as the ratio of the maximum image amplitude of the defect to the root-mean-square amplitude of the grain scattering noise in the image. In this paper, a general model for the SNR is presented, incorporating both single- and multiple-scattering components, which is valid across all ultrasonic scattering regimes. This model is used to quantitatively analyse array imaging performance and provides the foundation for formulating quantitative guidelines that relate image SNR to array system parameters. The results are illustrated using both simulated and experimental data.
