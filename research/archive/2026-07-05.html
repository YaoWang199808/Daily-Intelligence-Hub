Identification of the critical angle of repose of cohesive granular materials by continuous heap measurements

Authors: Adam Kolusz, Alberto Gallina, Przemysław Młynarczyk

Institution: AGH University of Krakow, Cracow University of Technology

Published: 2026-07-01

Journal: Measurement

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

The angle of repose (AR) is a fundamental parameter in the characterization of granular material, crucial for both terrestrial and extraterrestrial applications. This angle is defined as the maximum angle that a steady pile of granular material can form with the horizontal surface on which it lies. Traditional methods identify the angle from the analysis of one or more images of the formed pile. However, because of the highly non-linear behavior of the pile formation process, these methods often suffer from significant uncertainty in the measurement, particularly when the granular material possesses cohesive properties. This study introduces a novel measurement technique that addresses this problem and mitigates it. A custom-designed test stand, equipped with synchronized cameras, records the entire pile formation process. Then, a multi-step analytical procedure creates time series of the angle of the pile by processing all frames of the recordings, detects instances when the pile is in steady conditions, and identifies the angle of repose. Through this method, we can effectively isolate critical steady states of the pile occurring during the pile formation process, and precisely estimate the angle through a few test replicates. Experiments carried out on several lunar regolith simulants indicate that the proposed method can improve the accuracy of the AR estimate and reduce the measurement uncertainty.

Behavior of monopile-supported offshore wind turbines under normal operational and extreme-parked conditions in loose sand

Authors: Qasim Abu-Kassab, Richard Sause, James M. Ricles, Muhannad T. Suleiman, Arindam Banerjee, Kevin Wyckoff

Institution: Lehigh University

Published: 2026-07-04

Journal: Engineering Structures

Method: Hybrid

Citations: 0

Abstract:

Enhancing the performance and ensuring the long-time reliability of Offshore Wind Turbines (OWTs) requires a comprehensive understanding of OWT system response and interactions between OWT system components under realistic loading conditions. This paper presents Real-Time Hybrid Simulation (RTHS) results for a monopile-supported OWT under normal and extreme loading scenarios, which include aero-hydro-mechanical-geotechnical-structural component interactions. RTHS divides the OWT into analytical and experimental substructures, with dynamic equilibrium and compatibility maintained at the interfaces between substructures. The analytical substructures model the superstructure of the OWT above the mudline, along with the applied wave and wind loads, and the effects of the power-take-off unit using well-established numerical models, while the experimental substructure is the soil-monopile system that is physically tested in the laboratory. The RTHS results reveal that the OWT exhibits a ratcheting response due to a progressive accumulation of the monopile displacement under cyclic and dynamic loads. In addition, the stiffness and damping characteristics of the soil-monopile system change significantly, where the lateral stiffness increased by more than 100% and the damping ratio decreased by 76% relative to initial values during the RTHS. The results show that the OWT system response is dominated by wave loading and influenced by the soil-monopile response. The 1st fore-aft tower-monopile mode frequency was reduced by about 10% relative to a reference case without soil-monopile effects. The study shows that the soil-monopile response must be considered to accurately simulate the response of OWTs to various loading conditions.
